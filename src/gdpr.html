GDPR & Data Privacy

How AdminWorks collects, uses, and protects your personal data — in full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and its applicable national implementations.

1Who We Are

AdminWorks Ltd. ("AdminWorks", "we", "us", "our") is a company registered in Romania, operating from Calea Șerban Vodă 133, Central Business Park, Bucharest. We provide outsourced administrative and back-office services to businesses across the European Union and internationally.

For the purposes of GDPR, AdminWorks acts as both a Data Controller (when processing data about website visitors, prospects, and our own employees) and a Data Processor (when handling personal data on behalf of client businesses as part of service delivery).

Data Protection contact: dpo@adminworks.eu

2Legal Framework

This policy is governed by the following legislation and guidelines, all currently in force as of March 2026:

  • Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), in force since 25 May 2018
  • Romanian Law No. 190/2018 — national implementing measures for GDPR in Romania
  • ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC (as amended) — governing electronic communications and cookies
  • EDPB Guidelines — binding guidelines issued by the European Data Protection Board, including the latest guidelines on consent (05/2020), data transfers (05/2021), and legitimate interests (01/2024)
  • EU-US Data Privacy Framework (effective July 2023) — replacing the invalidated Privacy Shield for transatlantic data transfers

📌 Note: The EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689), which came into full application in February 2025, imposes additional transparency obligations where AI-assisted processing affects individuals. AdminWorks complies with all applicable provisions of the AI Act in its use of automated tools.

3Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

From website visitors and enquiries:

  • Name, work email address, company name, and phone number (submitted via contact forms)
  • IP address, browser type, device type, and browsing behaviour (collected via cookies and server logs)
  • Communication records — emails and messages sent to us

From clients and their authorised users:

  • Account credentials and login identifiers
  • Billing and invoicing information (name, address, VAT number)
  • Task instructions, business documents, and any personal data contained within delegated work

We do not collect: special categories of data (health, biometric, political opinions, etc.) unless explicitly required by a specific client engagement and covered by a separate Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

4Legal Basis for Processing

Under Article 6 GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to perform our services under a client contract or to take steps prior to entering into one.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Processing for business development, fraud prevention, IT security, and service improvement — where our interests are not overridden by your rights (assessed via a Legitimate Interest Assessment).
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Processing required to comply with Romanian and EU law, including tax, accounting, and anti-money laundering obligations.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Where required — specifically for non-essential cookies and marketing communications. Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. You may withdraw it at any time.

5How We Use Your Data

Personal data is used for the following purposes:

  • Delivering contracted services and communicating about ongoing tasks
  • Managing client accounts, billing, and invoicing
  • Responding to enquiries and booking consultations
  • Sending service updates and operational notifications
  • Marketing communications (only where you have opted in or where the soft opt-in rule under ePrivacy applies to existing clients)
  • Improving our website and services through aggregated analytics
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

6Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject under GDPR (Articles 15–22), you have the following rights. All requests are handled within 30 days, free of charge.

📋 Right of Access (Art. 15)

Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you, along with details of how it is processed.

✏️ Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data without undue delay.

🗑️ Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

Request deletion of your data where there is no longer a lawful reason for us to hold it ("right to be forgotten").

⏸️ Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

Ask us to pause processing of your data while accuracy or legitimate grounds are being assessed.

📦 Right to Portability (Art. 20)

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format to transfer to another provider.

🚫 Right to Object (Art. 21)

Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes at any time.

🤖 Rights re. Automated Decisions (Art. 22)

Not to be subject to solely automated decisions that produce significant legal or similar effects.

↩️ Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.

To exercise any right, contact us at dpo@adminworks.eu. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing the request.

7Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the stated purpose or as required by law:

  • Client account data: retained for the duration of the contract plus 5 years, in line with Romanian commercial law requirements
  • Invoicing and financial records: 10 years, as required by Romanian Fiscal Code
  • Enquiry and contact form data: 24 months from last contact if no contract is entered
  • Website analytics data: 14 months (aggregated and anonymised thereafter)
  • Employee records: duration of employment plus 5 years

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with our Data Disposal Policy.

8International Data Transfers

AdminWorks is based in Romania, an EU member state. All data processed within the EU/EEA benefits from GDPR protection by default.

Where data is transferred outside the EEA, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place:

  • Adequacy decisions: Transfers to countries recognised by the European Commission as providing adequate protection (e.g. UK, Switzerland, Japan, South Korea)
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): The 2021 Commission-approved SCCs are used for all other third-country transfers, supplemented by a Transfer Impact Assessment where required
  • EU-US Data Privacy Framework: For transfers to certified US recipients, in accordance with the adequacy decision of 10 July 2023

9Third-Party Processors

We use a limited number of trusted sub-processors to deliver our services. All are bound by Data Processing Agreements and comply with GDPR:

  • Microsoft 365 — email, document storage, and collaboration (EU data residency)
  • Google Workspace — productivity and scheduling tools
  • Stripe — payment processing (EU-US DPF certified)
  • Cloudflare — CDN and DDoS protection (EU-US DPF certified)
  • Plausible Analytics — privacy-first, cookie-free website analytics (EU-hosted)

We do not use advertising networks or data brokers. Our full sub-processor list is available on request.

10Cookies

We use the following categories of cookies:

  • Strictly necessary: Required for the website to function (session management, security). No consent required under ePrivacy Directive.
  • Analytics (optional): Privacy-first, cookieless analytics via Plausible — no personal identifiers are stored, no consent banner is required.
  • Functional (optional): Remember your preferences (e.g. language). Require consent.

We do not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies. You can manage preferences at any time via your browser settings or by contacting us.

11Data Breach Procedures

In the event of a personal data breach, AdminWorks will:

  • Contain and assess the breach within 24 hours of discovery
  • Notify the Romanian supervisory authority (ANSPDCP) within 72 hours, as required by Article 33 GDPR, where the breach poses a risk to individuals
  • Notify affected data subjects without undue delay where there is a high risk to their rights and freedoms (Article 34 GDPR)
  • Document all breaches in our internal Data Breach Register, regardless of whether notification is required

If you believe your data has been compromised, contact us immediately at security@adminworks.eu.
